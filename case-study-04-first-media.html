Overview

The Challenge

First Media — one of Indonesia's largest cable TV and internet service providers — needed a sustained digital lead acquisition engine that could reliably deliver high-volume qualified leads every single day, not just a one-time spike.

The initial target was ambitious: 300 qualified leads per day. In ISP marketing, this is a significant volume requiring precise multi-channel orchestration, consistent creative refresh cycles, and constant optimization to prevent ad fatigue.

The real test wasn't just hitting the target — it was sustaining and doubling it across 4 consecutive months without audience burnout or CPL degradation.

My Role

What I Did

Led the complete acquisition strategy from brief through to sustained delivery:

→ Multi-channel campaign architecture (Meta, TikTok, Google)
→ Lead volume forecasting and daily pacing management
→ Creative rotation strategy to prevent ad fatigue
→ Audience expansion and lookalike modeling
→ Weekly performance reviews with First Media stakeholders
→ Month-over-month optimization to scale from 300 → 600/day

Acquisition Strategy

How We Scaled

01
Multi-Channel Lead Architecture
Built a diversified lead ecosystem across Meta, TikTok, and Google Search — each serving a different audience intent state. Meta captured interest-level audiences with video lead ads, TikTok engaged younger demographics with native content, and Google captured high-intent searchers. No single channel dependency meant no single point of failure.
02
Creative Rotation System
Developed a 2-week creative refresh cadence to prevent ad fatigue — the #1 killer of sustained high-volume campaigns. New creative variants were batched, tested in week 1, and promoted to primary in week 2 if they matched or beat the control. This kept frequency from degrading CTR over the 4-month run.
03
Lookalike Audience Expansion
Started with First Media's existing subscriber list as the seed audience on Meta and TikTok, building 1–3% and 3–7% lookalike audiences. As M2 data matured, expanded to value-based lookalikes seeded from highest-quality lead cohorts. This expansion was the key driver behind the M2→M3 volume jump.
04
Daily Volume Pacing & QA
Built a daily pacing dashboard tracking leads against targets in real-time. If a channel underperformed before noon, budget was reallocated to the overperformer same-day. This operational discipline — not just strategic planning — is what kept 600/day consistent across all 30 days of Months 3 and 4.
Channel Mix

Where Leads Came From

Meta Ads
Primary Volume Driver
Facebook and Instagram Lead Ads served as the highest-volume channel. Native lead forms — requiring no landing page — reduced friction and maximized conversion rate from impression to submitted lead. Lookalike audiences from First Media's customer database drove targeting precision.
TikTok Ads
Growth & Demographic Expansion
TikTok targeted younger urban homeowners and apartment renters — a key growth segment for First Media. Click-to-WhatsApp format supplemented native lead forms. TikTok's contribution grew from 15% to 30% of total leads between M1 and M4 as creative quality matured.
Google SEM
High-Intent Capture
Google Search targeted the highest-intent searchers — those actively looking for cable TV or fiber internet in First Media service areas. Smaller volume but highest lead quality score. Provided a quality anchor that balanced the higher-volume but lower-intent social media leads.
Takeaways

Key Learnings

🏗️
Sustained volume requires systems, not just campaigns
Anyone can spike lead volume for a week. Maintaining 600+ leads/day for 120 consecutive days requires a different mindset — creative rotation systems, daily pacing dashboards, multi-channel redundancy, and a willingness to make same-day tactical decisions. The campaign infrastructure was as important as the campaign strategy.
🔄
Creative fatigue is a volume killer — rotation saves campaigns
At high frequency and sustained spend, the same creative wears out audiences within 2-3 weeks. Implementing a disciplined 2-week creative refresh cadence — with new variants continuously tested in rotation — was the primary reason CTR and CPL remained stable across all 4 months rather than degrading.
📡
Multi-channel redundancy protects volume targets
Running concurrent campaigns on Meta, TikTok, and Google meant that if any single platform experienced policy issues, algorithm shifts, or creative fatigue, the others could compensate. Single-channel dependency is a risk that large-volume campaigns cannot afford. Diversification isn't just about reach — it's about reliability.
