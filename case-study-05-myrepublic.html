Overview

The Challenge

MyRepublic — a fast-growing fiber internet provider — needed Google Ads Search to do something most brands stop short of: go beyond lead generation and drive actual direct sales. Not just form submissions. Real, signed-up subscribers.

The ask was straightforward but demanding: run a 2-month SEM campaign with a lean IDR 7 juta budget and measure success all the way to the bottom of the funnel — from search impression to active customer account.

In a market where ISP SEM campaigns typically report CPL and call it a day, tracking cost-per-sale raised the accountability bar entirely.

My Role

What I Did

Full-funnel Google Ads ownership with direct sales as the north star metric:

→ Campaign architecture: keyword research, match types, ad group structure
→ RSA ad copy writing and A/B testing (3+ variants per group)
→ Bid strategy management with conversion goal anchored to sales
→ Negative keyword expansion to protect IDR 3K CPC efficiency
→ Landing page alignment recommendations for conversion lift
→ Full-funnel reporting: Impressions → Clicks → Leads → Active Sales
→ Geographic targeting scoped to MyRepublic service coverage areas

Cost Efficiency

Budget Breakdown That Actually Matters

Cost Per Click
IDR 3K
Average CPC across the full 2-month campaign. For competitive ISP keywords — where larger telcos routinely bid IDR 5K–15K — maintaining IDR 3K CPC is a direct result of Quality Score discipline: tight keyword relevance, strong ad copy, and landing page alignment.
IDR 7.000.000 ÷ 2.100 clicks = IDR ~3.333 CPC
Cost Per Active Sale
IDR 87.5K
This is the number that matters. IDR 87,500 to acquire one active subscriber — a customer with ongoing monthly recurring revenue. For a fiber internet product priced at hundreds of thousands per month, this cost-per-acquisition represents exceptional ROI and a payback period of less than one billing cycle.
IDR 7.000.000 ÷ 80 sales = IDR 87.500 per customer
Cost Per Lead
IDR 17.5K
400 qualified leads from IDR 7M budget gives a CPL of IDR 17,500 — well below the ISP SEM benchmark of IDR 30K–50K/lead. The 19% click-to-lead rate also signals strong landing page alignment with search intent.
IDR 7.000.000 ÷ 400 leads = IDR 17.500 CPL
Lead-to-Sale Conversion
20%
1 in 5 leads became an active subscriber. For ISP direct sales — where leads often require multiple follow-ups, address verification, and installation scheduling — a 20% lead-to-sale rate indicates the campaign was attracting genuinely high-intent customers, not just casual inquiries.
80 sales ÷ 400 leads = 20% conversion rate

Approach

SEM Strategy Built for Sales, Not Just Leads

01
Sales-Intent Keyword Selection
Focused keyword selection on terms that signal purchase intent, not just research. "Daftar MyRepublic", "pasang fiber MyRepublic", "promo internet MyRepublic" vs. informational queries like "internet fiber apa saja". This selection bias toward commercial intent is the foundational reason the 20% lead-to-sale rate was achievable.
02
Quality Score Optimization for CPC Control
Maintained IDR 3K average CPC in a competitive ISP market by obsessing over Quality Score: ad copy that precisely matched keyword intent, ad groups with tight thematic clustering (max 5–8 keywords per group), and ongoing feedback to align landing page messaging with Search queries. Higher Quality Score = lower CPC from Google's auction.
03
Full-Funnel Conversion Tracking
Set up Google Tag Manager and Google Ads conversion tracking to capture multiple funnel events: form submission (lead), phone call click, and — critically — sale confirmation (passed from CRM). This granular tracking enabled bid optimization toward actual sales signals, not just lead form completions.
04
Ad Scheduling & Geo-Fence Targeting
Applied bid adjustments to boost presence during peak search hours (7–9 AM commute, 7–10 PM evening browsing) and restricted geographic targeting to MyRepublic's serviceable coverage zones. Both levers reduced wasted spend and improved the relevance of every click — directly contributing to the 19% click-to-lead rate.

Takeaways

Key Learnings

💰
Track to sale, not just lead — it changes everything
When a campaign is optimized toward form submissions, the algorithm chases form fillers — not buyers. Integrating CRM sale data back into Google Ads conversion tracking (via offline conversion import) allows the bidding algorithm to learn what a high-quality, close-able lead looks like. The 20% lead-to-sale rate is partly a result of this signal quality — the algorithm was attracting real buyers.
📉
IDR 3K CPC in ISP SEM is achievable — but only with Quality Score discipline
Competitors in ISP SEM routinely pay IDR 5K–15K per click because they treat Google Ads as a fire-and-forget channel. Achieving IDR 3K average CPC required tight keyword grouping, ad copy that matched search intent precisely, and regular account hygiene. Every 1,000 points of Quality Score improvement translates directly to CPC reduction — and budget efficiency.
📍
Geographic precision multiplies budget efficiency for ISPs
Clicks from areas MyRepublic can't serve are 100% wasted spend. Restricting targeting to serviceable coverage zones — and applying bid modifiers to boost within high-density service areas — was a foundational setup decision. It's invisible in the final numbers, but it's what made IDR 17.5K CPL possible at a lean IDR 7M total budget.
🔼
Month 2 improvement proves the compound effect of SEM optimization
CPC dropped from IDR 3.5K to IDR 2.6K from Month 1 to Month 2 — a 26% improvement — while lead-to-sale rate climbed from 18% to 22%. This compound improvement is characteristic of well-managed SEM: the account learns, negatives expand, copy sharpens, and Smart Bidding accumulates signal. The best campaign performance is always in Month 3 onward — which is why continuity matters.
