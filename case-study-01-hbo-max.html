Overview

The Challenge

HBO Max entered Indonesia's fiercely competitive streaming market needing to cut through noise and build genuine brand awareness among digital-native audiences across multiple age groups and content preferences.

The brief was clear: run a cross-platform digital awareness campaign that maximizes reach while maintaining meaningful conversion efficiency — proving that awareness campaigns don't have to sacrifice measurable performance.

The campaign ran simultaneously across YouTube, Meta (Facebook & Instagram), and TikTok, each requiring distinct creative approaches, audience segmentation strategies, and optimization logic.

My Role

What I Did

As the lead digital strategist, I planned, executed, and optimized every layer of this campaign — from initial audience architecture to ongoing bid strategy adjustments.

This included:

→ Platform-specific creative briefs and audience segmentation
→ Budget allocation logic across three channels
→ Daily monitoring and performance-based reallocation
→ TikTok creative testing and CTR optimization
→ Cross-platform attribution analysis and reporting

Strategy

How We Approached It

01
Audience Architecture
Built distinct audience layers for each platform — TikTok focused on interest-based targeting for 18–34 year olds; Meta used lookalike audiences built from streaming-affinity signals; YouTube targeted intent-based segments via Google's affinity and in-market audiences.
02
Creative-Platform Fit
Worked with the creative team to develop platform-native formats: short-form vertical hooks for TikTok with clear CTAs, episodic content previews for YouTube pre-roll, and static + carousel variants for Meta optimized for thumb-stopping visuals.
03
Dynamic Budget Reallocation
Monitored performance daily and shifted budget toward TikTok once early signals showed exceptional CTR performance (above 8% within day 3). This data-driven reallocation compounded results significantly.
04
Conversion Optimization Loop
Ran A/B tests on CTA copy, landing page variants, and creative hooks weekly. TikTok's pixel integration allowed granular event tracking — enabling optimization down to the IDR 159 cost-per-conversion benchmark.

Takeaways

Key Learnings

🎯
TikTok is a conversion machine for entertainment brands
For content-led brands like streaming services, TikTok's native video format creates unmatched intent alignment. A CTR of 10.78% — vs. the 1-2% platform average — confirms that when creative matches the platform's culture, performance follows. Awareness and conversion aren't trade-offs; they're the same motion done right.
📊
Dynamic budget reallocation beats set-and-forget strategies
The single highest-impact decision was watching early signals and doubling down on TikTok within the first week. Real-time budget reallocation — informed by daily CTR and CPM monitoring — was the lever that drove the IDR 159 cost-per-conversion result.
🔁
Multi-platform campaigns require platform-native thinking
Repurposing one creative across all three platforms would have killed performance. Each platform needed its own hook, format, and call-to-action logic. Meta needed aspirational static visuals; TikTok needed personality and fast cuts; YouTube needed story-led previews that justified the 30-second view.
